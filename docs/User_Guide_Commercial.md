# Nex-Gen Lumina — Commercial User Guide

Welcome to Lumina for your business. Permanent residential and commercial lighting that works as hard as you do — and this guide is how you make it work for your property, your brand, and your customers. Your installer has already handled the hardware and the network; this guide is about running the lights day to day.

## What you'll need

- The Lumina app on your phone or tablet
- Your **email and temporary password** from your installer (you'll change the password on first sign-in)
- A few minutes to walk through the onboarding wizard if this is your first time
- Your team's email addresses if you want to invite managers or staff

---

## 1. Signing in

Your installer handed off a Lumina account at install time.

1. Open the **Lumina** app
2. Tap **Sign In**
3. Enter the **email** and **temporary password** your installer provided
4. You'll be prompted to **create a new password** — pick something secure
5. After sign-in, the app recognizes your commercial account and loads the multi-zone dashboard

<div class="tip">
<strong>Tip:</strong> If you didn't receive credentials, call your installer directly. They can generate a new account or resend the invitation.
</div>

## 2. Commercial onboarding — 8 quick steps

When your account is first set up (or when you add a new location), you'll walk through an 8-step wizard that tailors the app to your business. It takes about 10 minutes.

### Step 1: Business type

Pick what describes your business best — retail, restaurant, bar, office, hotel, mixed-use. This shapes the default settings and day-part suggestions to fit your industry.

### Step 2: Brand identity

Set up your brand colors. These become quick presets throughout the app and can be pushed to all your locations.

### Step 3: Hours of operation

Define business hours per day. The app uses them to build default schedules and decide when lights should be active.

### Step 4: Channels

Configure your lighting channels. Commercial properties typically have several:

| Channel role | Example use |
|-------------|-------------|
| Interior | Indoor ambient |
| Outdoor Facade | Building exterior |
| Window Display | Storefront windows |
| Patio | Outdoor dining/seating |
| Canopy | Covered outdoor structures |
| Signage / Accent | Signs and architectural accents |

Each channel has a **Coverage Policy** (Always On, Smart Fill, Scheduled Only) and a **Daylight Mode** (Soft Dim, Hard Off, Disabled).

### Step 5: Your teams

Add team members and assign roles. See **Roles and Permissions** (Section 12) for the four-tier role system.

### Step 6: Day parts

Automate lighting transitions through the day:

| Day part | Typical hours | Example use |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Morning / Open | 6 AM – 11 AM | Bright, welcoming |
| Midday | 11 AM – 2 PM | Full brightness, natural feel |
| Afternoon | 2 PM – 5 PM | Maintain visibility |
| Evening / Dinner | 5 PM – 9 PM | Warm, ambient mood |
| Late Night / Close | 9 PM – Close | Dimmed, winding down |

Day parts transition your lighting automatically — no manual taps required.

### Step 7: Multiple locations

If you manage multiple locations, add them here. Each one gets its own schedule, controllers, and team assignments. Single-location businesses can skip this.

### Step 8: Review and go live

Final check on all settings. Tap **Go Live** to activate your lighting system. You can adjust anything later from the **System** tab.

<div class="tip">
<strong>Tip:</strong> You can re-run any part of onboarding from <strong>System → Settings</strong> if you need to reconfigure business type, channels, or day parts.
</div>

## 5. Zone control — the real power of commercial

Commercial systems split your property into independent **zones** — things like "Storefront," "Patio," "Parking Lot," or "Building Perimeter." Each zone can be controlled separately or together.

### The Channel Selector Bar

Anywhere you apply patterns or adjust settings, you'll see a **Channel Selector Bar** at the top. It's how you target specific zones.

| Selection | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| **All** | Commands apply to every zone simultaneously |
| **Individual zone** (e.g., "Storefront") | Commands apply only to that zone |

**How to use it:**

1. Tap a zone name in the Channel Selector Bar
2. The bar highlights your selection
3. Any change you make now targets only that zone
4. Tap **All** to control the whole property again

### Each zone stands on its own

What you can do with independent zone control:

- Run an animated holiday pattern on the storefront while the parking lot stays on steady bright white
- Dim the patio at closing without affecting the building perimeter
- Schedule different on/off times for each zone based on its purpose

### Multi-controller zones

If a zone spans multiple controllers (common for larger buildings), those controllers stay in sync automatically — no action from you. Your installer configured it at setup.

<div class="tip">
<strong>Tip:</strong> If a portion of a zone ever falls out of sync, contact your installer — that's a hardware config matter, not something you adjust from the app.
</div>

## 6. Lumina AI — your zone-aware assistant

Lumina understands natural language and knows your zones by name.

### Reaching Lumina

- **Lumina tab** (center of the bottom bar) for full-screen chat
- **Chat bar** at the bottom of the dashboard for quick commands

### Typing or speaking

Type or tap the **microphone icon** to speak. Voice commands auto-submit after a 2-second pause.

### Zone-aware commands

| Say this | Lumina does this |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Turn on warm white" | Applies warm white to every zone |
| "Turn on the storefront" | Powers on only the Storefront zone |
| "Set patio to 50% brightness" | Adjusts patio brightness only |
| "Christmas theme on the storefront, bright white on parking lot" | Applies different settings to each zone |
| "Turn off everything except the parking lot" | Selective zone control |
| "Brighten up the patio" | Increases patio brightness |
| "What's running on the storefront?" | Reports current status for that zone |
| "Schedule warm white on the patio at sunset" | Creates a zone-specific schedule |
| "Turn off all lights" | Powers down every zone |

<div class="tip">
<strong>Tip:</strong> Lumina learns your zone names and your preferences over time. Use it regularly and it gets sharper.

## 7. Scheduling

The **Schedule** tab automates your lighting on a weekly basis. Commercial schedules usually revolve around business hours and can be set per zone.

### Viewing your schedule

1. Tap **Schedule**
2. The week view shows each day with its scheduled patterns
3. Tap a day to see details — pattern name, times, zones affected
4. Use arrows to move between weeks

### Creating a schedule

1. On the Schedule tab, tap **+**
2. Pick:
   - **Zone** — which zone this schedule applies to, or **All** for everything
   - **Pattern/Scene** — the lighting to display
   - **Start Time** — clock time, or Sunrise/Sunset with an offset
   - **End Time** (optional)
   - **Repeat Days** — days of the week
3. Tap **Save**

Schedules live on the controllers themselves — they run even when the app is closed and you're off-site.

### Typical business-hours setup

| Zone | Schedule | Days |
|------|----------|------|
| Storefront | Holiday pattern, sunset to 11 PM | Mon–Sun |
| Storefront | Warm white, 5:30 AM to sunrise | Mon–Sat |
| Patio | Warm white, sunset to closing (10 PM) | Mon–Sun |
| Parking Lot | Bright white, sunset to sunrise | Every day |
| Building Perimeter | Bright white, dusk to dawn | Every day |

<div class="tip">
<strong>Tip:</strong> Use sunset/sunrise offsets for schedules that should adapt to the season. "Sunset minus 15 minutes" keeps the lights coming on just before dark all year round.
</div>

### Auto-Pilot

Lumina's Auto-Pilot generates weekly schedule suggestions based on upcoming holidays, your preferences, and the season. You choose the autonomy level:

| Mode | Behavior |
|------|----------|
| **Ask Me First** | You approve each weekly plan |
| **Smart Suggestions** | Auto-applies after 24 hours if you don't respond |
| **Full Auto-Pilot** | Applies automatically, no approval needed |

You can always override Auto-Pilot by editing any scheduled item manually.

### Day-part automation

Day parts are a separate layer that handles lighting transitions throughout the business day. Unlike individual schedules, day parts define a continuous program covering the whole day.

**To configure:**

1. **System → Settings → Day Parts** (or configure during onboarding Step 6)
2. Define the time range for each day part
3. Assign a lighting preset to each day part
4. Day parts apply automatically every day

**Example restaurant:**

| Day part | Time | Lighting |
|----------|------|----------|
| Breakfast | 6:00 AM – 11:00 AM | Bright white, full brightness |
| Lunch | 11:00 AM – 2:00 PM | Warm white, 80% brightness |
| Afternoon | 2:00 PM – 5:00 PM | Warm white, 70% brightness |
| Dinner | 5:00 PM – 9:00 PM | Amber accent, 60% brightness |
| Close | 9:00 PM – 10:00 PM | Dim warm, 30% brightness |
| Overnight | 10:00 PM – 6:00 AM | Security lights only |

Day parts and manual schedules coexist. Manual overrides take priority, and the next day part resumes the auto program.

<div class="tip">
<strong>Tip:</strong> Day parts shine for businesses with consistent daily routines. Combine them with seasonal schedules for holidays and events — the manual schedule takes precedence during the event, day parts resume after.
</div>

## 9. White presets

Your system uses LEDs with a dedicated white channel, so whites come out clean and true — something standard color-only LEDs can't match.

### Built-in presets

| Preset | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Warm White** | Cozy, amber-tinted |
| **Soft White** | Gentle, slightly warm |
| **Natural White** | Balanced, true-to-life |
| **Cool White** | Clean, blue-tinted |
| **Bright White** | Pure white from the dedicated channel |

### Changing your defaults

1. **System → My Whites**
2. Tap a preset to preview it live
3. Pick your **Primary** (everyday default) and your **Complement**
4. Tap **Save**

### Custom white

Use the R, G, B, and W sliders to blend a custom white. The W slider controls the dedicated white channel independently, giving you precise color-temperature control.

## 11. Remote access — control from anywhere

Lumina lets you control your lights from anywhere — not just when you're on the property Wi-Fi.

### How it works

Your installer set up a small **bridge device** on your property network. It relays commands between the Lumina cloud and your local controllers. On the property Wi-Fi, the app talks to controllers directly for instant response. Off-site, commands route through the bridge. The app switches between modes automatically — you don't configure anything.

### Verifying

1. **System → Remote Access**
2. Confirm the connection mode
3. Confirm status shows **Connected** or **Bridge Online**
4. Tap **Test Bridge** any time to verify

<div class="warning">
<strong>Heads-up:</strong> If the bridge is offline, check that the bridge device at your property has power and is on the network. Contact your installer if the issue sticks around.
</div>

## 12. Roles and permissions

Commercial accounts use a 4-tier role system to control access across your organization.

### The roles

| Role | What they can do |
|------|------------|
| **Store Staff** | View-only access to their assigned location |
| **Store Manager** | Full control of their location — schedules, overrides, patterns |
| **Regional Manager** | Manage multiple locations, push to a region, manage users |
| **Corporate Admin** | Full access to everything — brand colors, corporate lock, push to all locations |

### Permission matrix

| Permission | Staff | Manager | Regional | Corporate |
|-----------|:-----:|:-------:|:--------:|:---------:|
| View own location | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| Edit schedules | — | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| Override now (manual control) | — | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| View all locations | — | — | ✓ | ✓ |
| Push to region | — | — | ✓ | ✓ |
| Push to all locations | — | — | — | ✓ |
| Apply corporate lock | — | — | — | ✓ |
| Manage users | — | — | ✓ | ✓ |
| Edit brand colors | — | — | — | ✓ |

### Corporate lock

When a Corporate Admin applies a lock, local managers cannot override the schedule during the locked period. Use this for brand-wide promotions, holidays, or corporate events.

1. **System → Corporate Lock**
2. Pick the schedule or pattern to lock
3. Set the lock period (start and end)
4. Tap **Apply Lock**
5. All affected locations get a notification

### Inviting a team member

1. **System → Manage Users**
2. Tap **Invite User**
3. Enter their email
4. Pick their **role** (Store Staff, Store Manager, Regional Manager, Corporate Admin)
5. If applicable, assign specific **locations** or **regions**
6. They get an invite to download Lumina and create their account
7. Once they accept, they appear in your user list with their assigned role

### Removing a user

1. **System → Manage Users**
2. Tap the user
3. Tap **Remove User** — access is revoked immediately

<div class="tip">
<strong>Tip:</strong> Give trusted shift managers or key staff the Store Manager role so they can adjust lighting without pulling you in. Use Store Staff for anyone who only needs visibility.
</div>

## 14. Brand identity and corporate push

Corporate Admins can enforce brand consistency across every location.

### Setting brand colors

1. **System → Settings → Brand Identity** (or during onboarding Step 2)
2. Define your primary and secondary brand colors
3. They appear as quick presets on every location's dashboard
4. Tap **Save**

### Pushing settings

- **Push to Region** — Regional Managers push a schedule or pattern to every location in their region
- **Push to All** — Corporate Admins push to every location company-wide
- Pushed settings override local schedules until the push expires or is removed

To push:

1. Select the pattern, schedule, or setting
2. Tap **Push to…** and pick **Region** or **All Locations**
3. Optionally set an **expiration date**
4. Confirm — affected locations get a notification

### Business profile

1. **System → Settings → Business Profile**
2. Edit business name, logo, contact info
3. These show on reports and notifications

<div class="warning">
<strong>Heads-up:</strong> Pushing to all locations overrides any local schedule changes. Give your location managers a heads-up before a company-wide push.
</div>

|-------------|------|

## 16. Connection status

The app shows a connection status so you always know where you stand:

| Indicator | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Green dot** | Connected — controllers responding normally |
| **Bridge icon (green)** | Connected remotely via the bridge |
| **"Reconnecting…"** | Temporary connection loss — the app is retrying automatically |
| **"System Offline"** | Can't reach any controller — check property power and network |

**If you see "System Offline" while on-site:**

1. Confirm your phone or tablet is on the **same Wi-Fi network** as the lighting system
2. Verify the controllers have power
3. Close and reopen the app

**If you see "System Offline" while off-site:**

1. The bridge at the property may be offline — check with on-site staff
2. Confirm the property has internet
3. Contact your installer if it sticks

## What success looks like

- Your dashboard loads and shows every zone online and responding
- Day parts transition automatically through the day without manual touches
- Auto-Pilot is delivering weekly schedules that match your brand and season
- Staff can make the changes they're supposed to make; can't make the ones they shouldn't
- Off-site remote access works — the bridge indicator stays green week over week
- Your team can describe what's running from a "Lumina, what's on the patio right now?" voice query without opening a screen

## If something isn't working

**"One zone isn't responding while the others are."**
Open the Channel Selector Bar — make sure that zone is selected (or switch to **All**). If the issue is with the lights themselves, a portion of the zone may have dropped off — contact your installer.

**"The app says 'System Offline' on-site."**
Your phone isn't on the same Wi-Fi as the lighting system. Switch networks, then close and reopen the app.

**"The app says 'System Offline' off-site."**
The bridge at your property may be offline. Have on-site staff check that the bridge device has power and is on the network. If persistent, contact your installer.

**"A manager can't edit the schedule."**
Check their role — Store Staff is view-only. Bump them to Store Manager in **System → Manage Users**.

**"A corporate push overrode a schedule I wanted to keep."**
Corporate pushes take precedence over local schedules until they expire. If it needs to change now, contact your Corporate Admin; they can remove the push.

**"A day part isn't transitioning."**
Confirm the time ranges in **System → Settings → Day Parts** don't overlap or leave gaps. If a manual schedule is running, it takes priority — day parts resume after.
