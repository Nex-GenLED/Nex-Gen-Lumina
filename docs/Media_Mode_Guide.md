# Nex-Gen Lumina — Media Mode

Media Mode gives you temporary, respectful access to any Lumina customer's lighting system so you can shoot great content — video, stills, promotional — without ever touching their saved settings, schedules, or account. It's the cleanest way to dial in the perfect look for a customer home, capture it, and leave their system exactly how you found it.

## What you'll need

- The Lumina app on your phone or tablet
- Your **6-character Media Access Code** (from your Nex-Gen LED LLC team lead)
- The customer's email or street address to find their system
- Permission from the customer for the content shoot (always)

---

## What you can do in Media Mode

### Find a customer system
Search by email or street address to locate the right home in the network.

### Control their lights
Adjust colors, patterns, brightness, and effects for the duration of your shoot. Browse the full pattern library via the **Explore** tab and preview effects live.

### View-only everywhere else
You can control lights during your session, but you cannot change schedules, settings, or account info. Customer data stays protected — that's the promise.

---

## About your access code

Your Media Access Code is a **6-character alphanumeric code** — something like `A7X2K9`.

- Codes are assigned by your Nex-Gen team lead
- Each team member has a unique code
- Codes may have expiration dates

---

## Getting in

1. Open the Lumina app
2. On the Link Account screen, tap **Media** under **Professional Access**
3. Review the Media Mode information
4. Tap **Enter Media Access Code**
5. Enter your 6-character code
6. The Media Dashboard opens

---

## Working from the Media Dashboard

### Check the bridge status first

When you enter a customer's system, the **Home** tab shows a bridge status indicator so you know the home's lights are reachable before you start shooting. Green means the bridge is online and ready. Amber or red means there's a connectivity issue that will affect your session — deal with it before you roll.

### Search for a customer

- Enter their email or street address
- Matching customer systems appear in the results
- Tap one to view that system

### "View As" mode

- When you open a customer's system, you see their Home dashboard
- Control their lights as if you were them
- A banner at the top reminds you that you're in View As mode
- Tap the X on the banner to exit and return to search

### What you can control

- Power on/off
- Brightness
- Pattern/effect (via the **Explore** tab)
- Color
- Speed and intensity

### What's off-limits

- Schedules (the **Schedule** tab is read-only)
- System settings (the **System** tab is locked)
- Customer account info
- Adding or removing controllers
- Inviting users

## Session rules

| Rule | What it means |
|------|---------|
| **Timeout** | Sessions expire after 4 hours of inactivity |
| **Audit log** | All access is logged — this is a feature, not something to worry about |
| **Courtesy** | Restore the original lighting when you're done |
| **Permission** | Make sure the customer has agreed to the shoot |

---

## Best practices

1. **Coordinate with Sales** — confirm the customer has agreed to content creation
2. **Check bridge status** — make sure it's green before you start
3. **Note the starting setup** — snap a photo of the dashboard, write down the pattern name, whatever it takes to restore it later
4. **Restore when done** — return lights to the customer's preferred state
5. **Be considerate with timing** — avoid late evenings unless arranged
6. **Flag issues** — if you notice something off with the system, send a note to support

---

## What success looks like

- Bridge status indicator was green the whole session
- You captured the content you came for — video, stills, or both
- The customer's lights are back to exactly how they were when you arrived
- The audit log shows a clean session with no failed commands

## If something isn't working

**"Invalid access code."**
Your code may be expired or typed wrong. Confirm the 6 characters with your team lead.

**"Cannot reach this customer's system."**
The bridge status will tell you why. If it's amber or red, the home's bridge is offline — you won't be able to shoot this session. Contact the customer or your team lead to figure out next steps.

**"Can't find the customer in search."**
Double-check the email or address spelling. If the customer's account was created recently, it may take a few minutes to appear in search — wait and retry.

**"A control isn't responding."**
If brightness or pattern changes stop registering, the bridge may have dropped. Pull to refresh the dashboard, and confirm the bridge indicator is still green.
